# Mentor
## Complete Project Vision & Philosophy Document

---

# WHAT IS MENTOR?

Mentor is a behavioral accountability and execution-awareness system.

It is designed to help people:

- become more self-aware
- improve consistency
- detect behavioral drift early
- reduce self-deception
- build stronger execution habits
- understand behavioral patterns over time

Mentor is NOT:

- a productivity app
- a motivational app
- an AI therapist
- a habit streak app
- a dopamine gamification system
- a life coaching platform
- a generic AI chatbot

Mentor is:

> A behavioral mirror.

Its purpose is to help users honestly observe:

- what they committed to
- what they actually did
- where they drifted
- why they drifted
- what behavioral patterns repeat over time

---

# WHY MENTOR WAS STARTED

Mentor started from personal frustration.

The core problems behind the project were:

- lack of discipline
- inconsistency
- procrastination
- difficulty starting work
- poor execution consistency
- emotional goal switching
- behavioral drift
- self-negotiation
- delayed action
- weak self-accountability

The realization behind Mentor was:

> The problem was not lack of knowledge.

The problem was:

```text
intention → execution breakdown
```

Meaning:

- goals existed
- plans existed
- awareness existed

But consistent execution repeatedly collapsed.

The project was started because existing systems failed to solve this properly.

---

Most productivity systems focus on:

- task management
- organization
- planning
- reminders

- gamification
- dashboards

But they fail to solve the deeper problem:

# behavioral consistency.

Most systems assume:

Mentor assumes the opposite.

It assumes:

- self-awareness weakens over time
- behavior becomes emotionally distorted

- users silently abandon their goals

Mentor exists to expose those patterns honestly.

# THE CORE IDEA

Mentor works around one central idea:

# behavioral commitments across time.

The user creates a fixed behavioral commitment.

Example:

```text
For the next 30 days:
- Deep work >= 4 hours
- Gym daily
- Smoking <= 2
- Sleep before 1 AM
```

The system then tracks:

- whether the user followed the commitment
- consistency over time
- missed days
- behavioral reflections
- recurring patterns

The goal is NOT perfection.

The goal is:

# honest awareness.

---

# TEMPORAL INTEGRITY

One of the most important ideas in Mentor is:

# temporal integrity.

This means:

- users cannot fake consistency
- users cannot rewrite behavioral history
- users cannot backfill missed days

If a user misses a day:

- the day is permanently missed
- the next login requires reflection
- the drift becomes visible

This is extremely important psychologically.

Most productivity apps allow users to:

- repair streaks
- backfill history
- maintain fake progress

Mentor intentionally avoids this.

Because:

```text
behavioral awareness requires truthful history
```

# HOW MENTOR WORKS

## Step 1 — Commitment Creation

The user creates a behavioral contract.

The commitment contains:

- duration
- daily rules
- measurable metrics

Example:

```text
30 Day Discipline Reset
```

Metrics:

```text
Deep work >= 4h
Sleep >= 7h
Gym = true
Smoking <= 2
```

---

## Step 2 — Daily Check-In

At the end of the day the user logs:

### Objective Metrics

Examples:

- deep work hours
- distractions
- smoking count
- sleep hours
- workout completed

---

### Subjective Reflection

Examples:

- what was avoided
- biggest win
- biggest failure
- emotional state
- journal entry

This creates:

# behavioral continuity over time.

---

## Step 3 — Missed Day Detection

If the user does not submit a DailyEntry:

- the day becomes permanently missed
- no retroactive entry allowed
- next login forces reflection

This creates:

# accountability through visibility.

---

## Step 4 — Weekly AI Review

AI analyzes:

- behavioral trends
- recurring drift
- consistency patterns
- correlations
- behavioral loops

Examples:

```text
Your missed days correlate with poor sleep.

You consistently avoid difficult work during late evenings.

High distraction days reduce deep work significantly.
```

The AI should:

- identify patterns
- summarize behavior
- increase awareness

The AI should NOT:

- emotionally manipulate
- pretend to diagnose psychology
- act like a therapist
- give fake motivational speeches

---
